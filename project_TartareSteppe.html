Editorila Design: Tartare Steppe

The Tartar Steppe, a book by Dino Buzatti, is a reflection on how great expectations turn into nothing but disappointment when there is lack of action and initiative. The design of the book plays with the concept of deceived expectations: the pages are blank. The text is hidden inside uncut pages, which applies that active effort is necessary to achieve something of value.
Rotis font family that includes sans-serif, semi- serif and serif fonts was chosen for the book. The font subtly progresses from simple and minimal (sans) to more detailed and complex (serif) in each spread of the book, symbolising expectations building up.
